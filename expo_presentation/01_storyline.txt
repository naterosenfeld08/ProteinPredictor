Expo Poster Storyline Lock
Project: petase-thermostability-benchmark

Final Presentation Title
Calibrated Structural Benchmarking for Protein Design:
From Noisy WT/Mutant Metadata to Decision-Ready Ranking Signals

Central Claim
By combining CASP-style structure metrics with a sequence-diff discovery fallback,
we converted unreliable WT/mutant metadata into an interpretable benchmark layer
that can directly improve design-stage decision quality.

One-Sentence Takeaway (board center)
Our pipeline reliably finds usable WT/mutant structure pairs and transforms
predicted-vs-experimental similarity into calibrated ranking signals for
protein design under realistic data quality constraints.

30-Second Hook
Protein design decisions are often made with incomplete trust in structure predictions.
We built and validated a benchmark workflow that compares predicted mutant structures
to experimental references, adds calibration controls, and feeds those signals back
into design ranking so decisions are evidence-driven, not just score-driven.
